## 📊 Telecom Customer Churn Analysis (Excel)



In today's world , almost everyone relies on telecom services

From daily calls to internet access, it’s part of our everyday lives. But behind this constant usage lies a silent challenge:

👉 Customers leave.

Not suddenly. Not loudly.  
But gradually — often without being noticed.

---

### 💡 Project Overview

This project explores customer churn from a human-centered perspective, focusing on understanding why customers disengage over time.

Rather than approaching this dataset as just numbers, I approached it as behavior:

- When do customers start losing interest?  
- What patterns signal early dissatisfaction?  
- And what factors quietly push them to leave?

---

### 🛠 Approach (Excel-Centered Analysis)

This analysis was built using Excel (Power Pivot & Pivot Tables) to:

- Structure and model the dataset  
- Create key performance indicators (KPIs)  
- Build a clean, insight-driven dashboard  
- Explore relationships between customer behavior and churn  

Excel allowed me to move from raw data into a clear, business-focused narrative

---

### 🔁 Cross-Tool Validation

While this project is presented in Excel, the same dataset was previously explored using other tools.

Interestingly:

👉 The insights remained consistent.

This reinforced an important idea:

> Strong analysis is not dependent on the tool — it is driven by clear thinking and structured exploration.

---

### 📈 Key Focus Areas

The dashboard highlights key drivers of churn, including:

- Customer Tenure → Identifying when churn is most likely  
- Contract Type → Understanding commitment vs flexibility  
- Premium Tech Support → Evaluating the impact of customer support  
- Customer Segmentation → Recognizing high-risk groups  

---

### 🔍 Key Insights

- Customers are most likely to churn within the first 6 months  
- Month-to-month contracts show higher churn risk  
- Lack of premium tech support increases likelihood of churn  
- Early-stage disengagement is a strong indicator of customer loss  

---

### ❤️ Human Perspective

This analysis highlights something important:

Churn is not just a number.

It reflects moments where:
- Expectations were not met  
- Support was not available  
- Or engagement was not sustained  

👉 Behind every churned customer is a story.
